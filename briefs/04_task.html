Subject: Housing Affordability and Short Term Lets Dashboard, 1995 to 2025

The Department for Levelling Up, Housing and Communities is commissioning an internal analytics dashboard to support evidence based understanding of housing affordability trends and the potential relationship with short term lets in selected UK cities.

Decision makers need a tool that combines residential transaction data with household income context, and can be extended using short term let listing data to highlight potential pressures on housing availability. The output should help users explore differences across regions and within major cities.

Analytical standard: This tool is designed for decision support. You should describe associations and patterns, and you should avoid causal claims. Clearly document how you combine datasets, how you define regional coverage, and how you handle missingness, boundary changes, and differences in granularity.

data available

  • GOV.UK Price Paid Data including sale price, date, property type, and postcode or locality from 1995 to 2025
  • Household income data suitable for constructing price to income ratios at an appropriate geographic level
  • Short term let listing data for selected cities, using Inside Airbnb data extracts
  • Listing attributes including location, nightly price, room type, availability, and host listing counts

project objectives

  • Describe long run housing price trajectories by region and property type
  • Quantify affordability using price to income ratios and highlight geographic disparities
  • Explore whether short term let prevalence aligns with high prices or affordability stress in selected cities
  • Support users with clear filtering and a city focused deep dive view where data coverage allows

dashboard modules and questions

module 1: housing price trajectories

  • Views: Time series of prices by region, focus cities where short term let data exists, breakdown by property type
  • Questions: Which regions have the fastest long term growth? Are there periods of sharp change such as 2008 or 2020? Are certain property types driving growth disproportionately?

module 2: can people afford to live here

  • Views: Median price vs median income, price to income ratio by region, ranked affordability stress view
  • Questions: Where has affordability deteriorated most? Is the north south divide narrowing or growing? Are increases in income keeping up with house prices anywhere?

module 3: housing prices vs short term let prevalence

  • Views: Median prices vs short term lets per housing stock, London borough view where available
  • Questions: Are high price areas also short term let hotspots? Are there exceptions where prices are high but listings are low? Is short term let prevalence higher in already unaffordable markets?
Suggested KPI: Price growth since 1995 by region and property type
Suggested KPI: Price to income ratio by region and change over time
Suggested KPI: Short term lets per 1,000 homes in selected cities
Suggested KPI: Share of entire home listings and high availability listings

expected deliverables

  • Interactive dashboard suitable for non-technical stakeholders, with clear defaults and plain language labels
  • Technical note (1 to 2 pages) covering data sources, joins, definitions, and limitations
  • Brief findings summary with 3 to 5 key patterns and suggested follow up analysis

success criteria

Success will be judged on clarity, interpretability, reproducibility, and analytical judgement. The best submissions will communicate what the data suggests, what it does not suggest, and how decision makers could use the dashboard responsibly when considering housing pressures and local context.
