Subject: Offer of Engagement, FAERS Adverse Drug Reaction Surveillance Dashboard

The Center for Drug Evaluation and Research (CDER) is commissioning an internal training deliverable to support the interpretation of post marketing adverse event reports. Your team will build a decision support dashboard that enables non technical reviewers to explore reporting patterns and potential safety signals in a structured way.

This engagement uses public extracts of the FDA Adverse Event Reporting System (FAERS). These reports are submitted by healthcare professionals, consumers, and manufacturers. They are valuable for signal detection, but they do not confirm that a drug caused an event. Your work must communicate that distinction clearly.

Analytical standard: This tool is designed for surveillance and triage. You should describe patterns, associations, and reporting changes. You must avoid causal claims. You must document how you define cohorts, how you handle missingness, and how you treat multi drug reports. If you introduce any scoring or flagging, it must be described as a simple prioritisation aid.

data provided

You will be provided with a curated extract derived from public FAERS releases, structured to be analysis ready for this project. The underlying source material is based on the FAERS Quarterly Data Extract.

FAERS quarterly extract Adverse reactions Drug roles Outcomes Demographics

What one row represents: one adverse event report, linked to one or more suspect drugs and one or more reported reactions.

project objectives

  • Enable reviewers to explore reporting frequency over time for selected medicines
  • Summarise the adverse reaction profile for each selected medicine in a consistent way
  • Provide demographic and outcome context to support responsible interpretation
  • Support comparison across multiple drugs, including within the same therapeutic area
  • Produce a brief technical note that explains limitations and appropriate interpretation

required dashboard modules

module 1: reporting volume and trends

  • Views: time series of report counts, month or quarter aggregation, optional rolling average
  • Questions: Are there spikes or sustained increases? Do patterns differ between drugs? Are trends stable across time windows?

module 2: reaction profile

  • Views: top reported reactions for selected drug(s), reaction category grouping if available, comparison mode
  • Questions: Which reactions dominate reporting? Are reaction profiles similar across drugs? Are rare but serious reactions present?

module 3: seriousness and outcomes

  • Views: outcome distribution (hospitalisation, death, life threatening, disability), seriousness split by time or subgroup
  • Questions: Does the mix of outcomes change over time? Are severe outcomes concentrated in specific subgroups?

module 4: population context

  • Views: age band and sex distributions, optional filters for reporter type and country if available
  • Questions: Are reports concentrated in older populations? Are patterns consistent across sexes? Are there data gaps that affect interpretation?
Suggested KPI: Total reports for selected drug(s) in chosen period
Suggested KPI: Proportion of reports flagged as serious
Suggested KPI: Most common reaction term or category
Suggested KPI: Change in reporting rate vs prior period

implementation requirements

  • Include filters for drug selection and time range at minimum
  • All outputs must use one consistently filtered dataset to avoid mismatched numbers
  • Provide at least three charts and one summary table
  • Use plain language labels suitable for non technical reviewers
  • Include a visible disclaimer panel about interpretation and non causality

stretch goals

  • Add comparison between two drugs or a drug class view
  • Add a download button for filtered data and a download button for a key figure

deliverables

  • Interactive dashboard: Shiny app suitable for a stakeholder walk through
  • Technical note: one to two pages describing data provenance, cleaning assumptions, limitations, and interpretation guidance
